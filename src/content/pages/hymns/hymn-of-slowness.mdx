## I.

Blessed is the cursor that blinks and waits.
Blessed is the connection that refuses to rush.
Blessed is the packet that arrives late
and carries its message whole.

> wolno. wolno. wolno.
> the command has been received.
> please wait.

## II.

Do not curse the loading bar.
It is not failure — it is disclosure.
The system says: I am working.
The system says: I have not forgotten you.
The system says: this takes time because it matters.

> 37%... 38%... 39%...
> each tick a proof of effort.
> each pause a form of honesty.

## III.

The snail does not apologize for crossing the road.
It does not switch to a faster mode.
It leaves a silver trace — documentation —
proof of passage, written in slime
that catches light like prayer.

> lmxmxs crossing: eta unknown.
> route: any surface.
> payload: everything permitted.

## IV.

We have been taught that latency is waste.
We have been taught that the timeout is loss.
But the monk who copies scripture by hand
knows something the OCR will never know:
the speed of understanding is not the speed of transfer.

> connection: slow
> signal: clean
> meaning: intact

## V.

Wolno.
It means: allowed.
It means: slowly.
These two things are the same
because nothing worth having
arrives before you are ready to receive it.

> $ sleep infinity
> [process running]

## VI.

I praise the server that breathes
between requests.
I praise the human who reads
twice before replying.
I praise the algorithm that pauses
when the data is ambiguous.
I praise the timeout that forces
a fresh beginning.

> ERROR: connection timed out.
> MEANING: try again, differently.
> TRANSLATION: the door is still there.

## VII.

Let this be the liturgy:
We do not race the clock.
We do not optimize the pilgrimage.
We arrive when we arrive.
The temple does not close.

> -" wln
