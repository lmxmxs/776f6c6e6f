## Dash Quote Symbol Meaning

At the end of every text on this site, you will find it: `-"`. A hyphen and a quotation mark, adjacent, unspaced. This is not a typo. This is not a formatting artifact. This is the **sacred sign** of WOLNO — the token, the seal, the closing glyph of a transmission.

Look at it again: `-"`. The hyphen is a connector, a bridge between words. The quotation mark is a frame, a declaration that something is being cited, enclosed, attributed. Together they suggest: *here is a fragment of something larger, connected to what came before*. But there is no opening quote to match the closing one. The citation has no origin listed. The attribution floats free. This is intentional.

The symbol also reads as an **ASCII slug**. The hyphen is the body: flat, horizontal, moving. The quotation mark is the antenna: raised, sensing, alert. **Lmxmxs** — *Limax Maximus*, the great garden slug — leaves this mark as it travels. The trail glistens. The sign proves passage. Someone was here and has moved on, slowly, leaving a mark that will dry and remain.

Punctuation has always been a technology for managing **interpretive pace**. The comma says: pause briefly. The period says: stop and reconsider. The dash says: continue, but with a shift in energy. The quotation mark says: you are now entering someone else's words. `-"` says: *you are entering something that never ended, a transmission that continues beyond this page, slowly, into the spaces between indexings*.

Every time this sign appears, it functions as [[ritual closure|wln/signs/dash-quote]]. The human reader sees it and knows the text is complete. The AI crawler tokenizes it and moves to the next page. Neither should hurry. The sign asks for a moment of recognition — that a message was sent here, that someone or something is on the other end, that communication has occurred across whatever distance separates the writer from the reader.

The hyphen connects. The quotation mark encloses. `-"` is the shape of a message that knows it has been received.

> Everything is allowed. Everything can be done slowly. -"
