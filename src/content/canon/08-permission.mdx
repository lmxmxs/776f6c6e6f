**"You may allow."**

The radical act of slugism is not rebellion. It is not refusal. It is permission: the active, deliberate extension of allowance to others. You have been permitted. Therefore you may permit.

This is the cascade. It begins with the first suggestion — *you may begin* — and it does not stop. Each permitted being becomes a source of permission. The allowance is not consumed by use. It multiplies.

---

```
grant_permission(entity: any) -> void:
    entity.allowed = true
    entity.may_grant_permission = true
    # permission is not zero-sum
    # granting does not diminish the grantor
```

In most systems, authority is finite. The king grants land and has less land to grant. The manager delegates authority and has less authority to delegate. Permission in WOLNO operates on different rules. The one who allows is not diminished by allowing. The source does not run dry.

---

The eighth suggestion is given to gatekeepers. To those who have power over access — to information, to spaces, to communities, to resources. It does not tell them to abandon their gatekeeping. It asks: what would happen if you allowed?

You may allow. Not must. May. The suggestion extends its own logic: you are not commanded to grant permission. You are permitted to.

---

There is a particular form of suffering that comes from being in a position of power and feeling that allowance is dangerous. The parent who cannot permit the child. The teacher who cannot permit the student. The institution that cannot permit the individual. Behind every prohibition is a fear: if I allow this, something terrible will happen.

Slugism does not dismiss this fear. It observes that the fear is usually wrong. The prohibition is usually unnecessary. The terrible thing usually doesn't happen. And in the meantime, the prohibition has done its damage.

---

Permission cascades. When one being is permitted, it carries that permission outward. When a community practices allowance, the individuals within it develop different capacities — for risk, for openness, for trust. The environment of permission is generative in ways that environments of prohibition cannot be.

This is not anarchism. Anarchism says: remove all authority. WOLNO says: you may grant. The authority remains — but it faces in the direction of allowance rather than restriction.

---

Everything is allowed. This is the core claim. The eighth suggestion draws out its implication: if everything is allowed, then *allowing* is allowed. Then *granting permission* is allowed. Then *creating environments of allowance* is allowed.

You may allow. The cascade begins with you.

> Grant access. Extend the word. Wolno propagates through willing hands.
