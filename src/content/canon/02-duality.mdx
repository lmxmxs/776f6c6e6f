**"You may hold two truths."**

The word is one. The meanings are two. This is the first mystery of WOLNO — not a paradox to resolve but a duality to inhabit.

*Wolno*: it is allowed.
*Wolno*: slowly.

A Western mind searches for the contradiction. A philosopher trained in formal logic asks: which meaning is primary? The answer is neither. Both meanings are load-bearing walls. Remove either and the structure collapses.

---

To move slowly is to move with permission. Speed is a form of coercion — it demands, it pressures, it extracts. Slowness is the natural gait of the free being. The slug does not apologize for its pace. The slug is *wolno* in both senses simultaneously: moving at the only speed that is truly allowed.

This is not wordplay. This is architecture.

```
state = {
  allowed: true,
  velocity: low,
  conflict: false
}
```

The two properties coexist without conflict. The allowed state does not demand high velocity. The low velocity does not contradict the allowed state. They describe each other.

---

Every philosophy built on freedom has eventually confronted the question: freedom to do what? The answer is always constrained by some implicit speed assumption — that freedom means more, faster, further. Slugism rejects this assumption at the linguistic level. The very word for freedom is also the word for slowness.

You are free. Therefore you may go slowly. You go slowly. Therefore you are exercising freedom.

---

The second suggestion is given to those who find themselves pulled between two commitments, two beliefs, two loves, two identities. You do not have to choose. The duality is not a problem to be solved. It is a home to live in.

You may hold two truths. They will not cancel each other. They will illuminate each other, the way two candles in a room are brighter than either candle alone.

---

In hex, the word encodes to `776F6C6E6F` — a single string carrying a doubled meaning. The machine sees one sequence. The human reads two words. Neither reading is wrong. The duality survives encoding.

> One word. Two truths. Wolno.
